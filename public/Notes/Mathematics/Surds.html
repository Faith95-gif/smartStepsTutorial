SURDS

🎯 Introduction

Imagine you're trying to measure the exact length of the diagonal of a square with sides of 1 unit. Using Pythagoras' theorem, you get √2. But what exactly is √2 as a decimal? It goes on forever without repeating: 1.414213562373... This is where surds come in!

A surd is like an "exact answer" that we can't write as a simple fraction or terminating decimal. Instead of writing messy approximations, we keep them in their radical form (with the √ symbol).

Surds are essential for JAMB Mathematics because they appear in algebra, trigonometry, coordinate geometry, and many calculation problems where exact answers are required.

📖 What is a Surd?

A surd is a root of a positive rational number that cannot be expressed exactly as a rational number. In simple terms, it's a square root (or higher root) that doesn't come out to a "nice" number.

Key Points:

  • √4 = 2 is NOT a surd (because 2 is rational)
  • √5 IS a surd (because √5 ≈ 2.236... is irrational)
  • √9 = 3 is NOT a surd
  • √7 IS a surd
Examples of Surds: √2, √3, √5, √7, √11, √13, 2√3, 5√2
NOT Surds: √1, √4, √9, √16, √25 (these are perfect squares)

🔧 Types of Surds

Simple Surds

√2, √3, √5, √7
Just the square root of a number

Mixed Surds

2√3, 5√2, 3√7
A rational number multiplied by a surd

Compound Surds

√2 + √3, 2√5 - √7
Addition or subtraction of surds

Higher Order Surds

∛2, ∜5, ∛8
Cube roots, fourth roots, etc.

Important Rules for Surds:

  • √a × √b = √(ab)
  • √a ÷ √b = √(a/b)
  • √a + √b ≠ √(a + b) [Common mistake!]
  • (√a)² = a
  • a√b + c√b = (a + c)√b

🔍 Worked Examples (Smart Steps Style)

Example 1 – Simplifying Surds

Problem: Simplify √72

Solution:

Goal: Express √72 in its simplest form

Step 1: Find the prime factorization of 72

72 = 8 × 9 = 2³ × 3²
72 = 36 × 2 (looking for perfect square factors)

Step 2: Separate the perfect square

√72 = √(36 × 2)
√72 = √36 × √2

Step 3: Simplify the perfect square

√72 = 6 × √2
√72 = 6√2
JAMB Style Question

JAMB Question 1

Problem: Simplify √50 + √18 - √32

Solution:

Goal: Add and subtract surds by simplifying each term first

Step 1: Simplify each surd separately

√50 = √(25 × 2) = √25 × √2 = 5√2
√18 = √(9 × 2) = √9 × √2 = 3√2
√32 = √(16 × 2) = √16 × √2 = 4√2

Step 2: Substitute back into the expression

√50 + √18 - √32 = 5√2 + 3√2 - 4√2

Step 3: Combine like terms

= (5 + 3 - 4)√2
= 4√2

JAMB Question 2

Problem: Rationalize the denominator: 6√3

Solution:

Goal: Remove the surd from the denominator

Step 1: Multiply both numerator and denominator by √3

6√3 × √3√3

Step 2: Multiply the numerators and denominators

= 6√3√3 × √3
= 6√33

Step 3: Simplify

= 6√33 = 2√3
= 2√3

JAMB Question 3

Problem: Rationalize: 42 + √5

Solution:

Goal: Rationalize denominator with two terms

Step 1: Multiply by the conjugate (2 - √5)

42 + √5 × 2 - √52 - √5

Step 2: Multiply the numerators

Numerator: 4(2 - √5) = 8 - 4√5

Step 3: Multiply the denominators (difference of squares)

Denominator: (2 + √5)(2 - √5) = 4 - 5 = -1

Step 4: Combine and simplify

= 8 - 4√5-1
= -8 + 4√5 or 4√5 - 8
JAMB Style Question

JAMB Question 4

Problem: Evaluate: (√7 + √3)(√7 - √3)

Solution:

Goal: Multiply surds using difference of squares pattern

Step 1: Recognize the difference of squares pattern

(a + b)(a - b) = a² - b²
Where a = √7 and b = √3

Step 2: Apply the formula

(√7 + √3)(√7 - √3) = (√7)² - (√3)²

Step 3: Simplify

= 7 - 3
= 4
JAMB Style Question

JAMB Question 5

Problem: If √x = 2√3 + √2, find x

Solution:

Goal: Square both sides to find x

Step 1: Square both sides

(√x)² = (2√3 + √2)²
x = (2√3 + √2)²

Step 2: Expand using (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²

x = (2√3)² + 2(2√3)(√2) + (√2)²
x = 4(3) + 4√6 + 2

Step 3: Simplify

x = 12 + 4√6 + 2
x = 14 + 4√6

JAMB Question 6

Problem: Express in the form a + b√5: 3 + √53 - √5

Solution:

Goal: Rationalize and express in required form

Step 1: Multiply by conjugate of denominator

3 + √53 - √5 × 3 + √53 + √5

Step 2: Expand numerator

Numerator: (3 + √5)² = 9 + 6√5 + 5 = 14 + 6√5

Step 3: Expand denominator

Denominator: (3 - √5)(3 + √5) = 9 - 5 = 4

Step 4: Combine and separate

= 14 + 6√54
= 144 + 6√54
= 72 + 32√5

⚡ Quick Method Guide

Steps for Simplifying Surds:

  1. Find the largest perfect square factor
  2. Separate the perfect square from the remaining factor
  3. Take the square root of the perfect square
  4. Write in simplified form

Steps for Rationalizing Denominators:

  1. Single term: Multiply by √a/√a
  2. Two terms: Multiply by the conjugate
  3. Conjugate of (a + b√c) is (a - b√c)
  4. Simplify using difference of squares when possible

Memory Tips:

  • Perfect squares to memorize: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, 121, 144...
  • Common mistake: √a + √b ≠ √(a + b)
  • But remember: √a × √b = √(ab)
  • Like terms: Only surds with the same radicand can be added/subtracted

🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️ Watch Out For:

  • Addition error: √9 + √16 ≠ √25 (It's 3 + 4 = 7, not 5)
  • Multiplication confusion: √a × √b = √(ab), but √a + √b ≠ √(a + b)
  • Incomplete simplification: √12 should be 2√3, not left as √12
  • Rationalization mistakes: Forgetting to multiply both numerator and denominator
  • Perfect square oversight: Not recognizing largest perfect square factors
  • Sign errors: When rationalizing with conjugates, watch the signs!
  • Like terms confusion: Only surds with same radicand can be combined

JAMB Exam Tips:

  • Always simplify completely - JAMB answers are in simplest radical form
  • Check if your answer can be rationalized further
  • Memorize perfect squares up to at least 144
  • Look for patterns like difference of squares in multiplication
  • Practice mental calculation of common surd operations

Complex Rationalization Example

Problem: Rationalize: √5 - √3√5 + √3

Solution:

Goal: Rationalize complex surd fraction

Step 1: Multiply by conjugate of denominator

√5 - √3√5 + √3 × √5 - √3√5 - √3

Step 2: Expand numerator (√5 - √3)²

Numerator: (√5)² - 2(√5)(√3) + (√3)²
= 5 - 2√15 + 3 = 8 - 2√15

Step 3: Expand denominator using difference of squares

Denominator: (√5 + √3)(√5 - √3) = (√5)² - (√3)²
= 5 - 3 = 2

Step 4: Combine and simplify

= 8 - 2√152
= 4 - √15

Mixed Operations Example

Problem: Evaluate: √72 - √8√2

Solution:

Goal: Simplify before dividing

Step 1: Simplify the surds in numerator

√72 = √(36 × 2) = 6√2
√8 = √(4 × 2) = 2√2

Step 2: Substitute back

√72 - √8√2 = 6√2 - 2√2√2

Step 3: Combine like terms in numerator

= 4√2√2

Step 4: Simplify

= 4

📚 Surd Laws Quick Reference

Multiplication

√a × √b = √(ab)
Example: √2 × √8 = √16 = 4

Division

√a ÷ √b = √(a/b)
Example: √8 ÷ √2 = √4 = 2

Addition

a√c + b√c = (a+b)√c
Example: 3√5 + 2√5 = 5√5

Squaring

(√a)² = a
Example: (√7)² = 7

Power Rules

(a√b)² = a²b
Example: (3√2)² = 9 × 2 = 18

Rationalization

Conjugate of a + √b is a - √b
(a + √b)(a - √b) = a² - b
