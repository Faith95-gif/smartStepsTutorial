üéØ Introduction

Imagine you open a delicious meat pie and see that the filling inside is part of a full round pie. Or think of slicing a large birthday cake into different portions. Each slice you take is a sector of a circle.

Understanding arcs and sectors is very important in JAMB Mathematics because they appear in geometry, mensuration, and even real-life measurement problems.

üìê What is a Sector?

A sector is a region inside a circle bounded by two radii and an arc.

  • Arc = curved edge of the sector
  • Two radii = straight lines from the center of the circle to the circumference

The angle between the two radii is called the angle subtended at the center, denoted by Œ∏ (theta).

üîÑ Types of Sectors

1. Minor Sector

  • Subtends an angle less than 180¬∞ at the center
  • Looks like a small slice of pizza or cake

2. Major Sector

  • Subtends an angle greater than 180¬∞ at the center
  • Occupies most of the circle, leaving the smaller part as the minor sector

Minor = smaller slice üçï

üåä What is an Arc?

An arc is a portion of the circumference of a circle.

  • Minor Arc ‚Üí The smaller curved part of the circle
  • Major Arc ‚Üí The larger curved part of the circle
